JoElla Carman

Greetings,

I am pleased to be applying for the role of visual information designer with Yahoo Finance. I am passionate about making complex topics intelligible and enabling people to make better decisions with data. I have years of experience making fluent, engaging graphics for news organizations to support these needs.

Currently I am part of the interactive storytelling team at National Geographic where I work with a broad portfolio of subject matter. I've helped craft design systems for series, one-off projects, and larger features, including an interactive on shifting global religious populations that earned an SND bronze medal, and contributed to two Webby- and SND-award-winning Pictures of the Year packages. Working with the internationally-recognized team at the magazine has sharpened my design sense and ability to articulate key decisions to stakeholders.

Previously I was a visual designer at NBC News Digital where I pitched and designed daily news graphics and lead larger initiatives such as the “Senate What If” interactive for the 2022 midterms. In that role I initiated the team’s style guide and specifications for custom design themes in Datawrapper, establishing shared visual standards and templates to keep output consistent as the team scaled.

I bring a sense of curiosity to all my work and a commitment to craft. I strive to meet users where they’re at with engaging experiences that are built on research and insight, while meeting tight deadlines and integrating with wider editorial needs. I would love to talk further about how my skills could help move things forward at Yahoo Financial.

Thank you for your consideration,
JoElla Carman
