Greetings,

I am pleased to be applying for the role of data journalist and visualization specialist with Booz Allen Hamilton. I am passionate about the role of data in public health and know that my skills and experience position me to discover and communicate valuable insights to the team and to the public.

Currently I am an interactive designer on the Data Graphics team at NBC News Digital, where I work on daily graphics, trackers, deep-dive stories, fun stuff, and other projects that aim for wide appeal. projects big and small. I also contribute story ideas, help shape narratives, design layouts and visuals, clean and analyze data, and execute the projects for the web.

I have years of experience developing both larger web apps and one-off graphics. I am highly skilled at front-end work generally (JS and CSS) and particularly fluent with D3 among other tools for charts and maps. I am also conversant with R, SQL, and advanced techniques in Google Sheets for managing data.

Visual journalism is a second chapter for me. After years of working in the news library at NPR, I set out to learn the crafts of design and web development to supplement what I knew about journalism and data. I have been able to learn largely by doing, working in an agency, non-profits, and newsrooms.

I’m looking for my next step to take me somewhere where I can collaborate with a larger team of folks who are as passionate about the work as I am. It would be an honor to be considered for this position.

Many thanks,
JoElla Carman
