About Me

I'm a creative developer with a background in news. I changed direction in my career because I got jealous of all the fun developers were having building the final package. My skills now include Ruby, Rails, JavaScript, jQuery, HTML/CSS, Backbone and PostgreSQL.

Until recently I worked as a librarian at NPR. Together with editorial staff, I gathered the various facts, data and multimedia tidbits that go into crafting a rich story experience.

Some of the editorial projects I worked on were @todayin1963 and Pop-Up Politics. I also wrote a few original works of my own.

I love the storytelling potential of structured data. Because I'm a DIY person by nature, I set out to learn to code. Now I am looking for the perfect opportunity to put these skills to use, finding important stories and coding them into elegant, memorable experiences.
